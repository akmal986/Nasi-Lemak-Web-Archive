Nasi Lemak Recipes

Discover the authentic flavors of Nasi Lemak with our collection of recipes. Learn how to prepare this iconic Malaysian dish and its popular variations right in your kitchen.

Nasi Lemak Biasa (Standard)

The essential recipe for fragrant coconut rice, spicy sambal, and classic accompaniments. Perfect for beginners and purists alike.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice, washed and drained
  • 2 cups thick coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 3-4 pandan leaves, knotted
  • 1 tsp salt
  • For Sambal: Dried chilies, shallots, garlic, belacan, tamarind paste, sugar, salt
  • Accompaniments: Fried ikan bilis, roasted peanuts, hard-boiled eggs, cucumber slices

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice with coconut milk, water, pandan leaves, and salt until fluffy.
  2. Prepare sambal by blending ingredients and simmering until fragrant and oil separates.
  3. Assemble with desired accompaniments.

Nasi Lemak Ayam Goreng Berempah

Learn to make crispy, aromatic spiced fried chicken to pair with your Nasi Lemak. A crowd-pleasing favorite!

Ingredients:

  • Chicken pieces
  • Lemongrass, ginger, galangal, turmeric (fresh), curry leaves, fennel seeds, cumin seeds, coriander powder
  • Flour (rice flour/cornstarch for crispiness)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate chicken with blended spices and powders for at least 2 hours.
  2. Lightly coat with flour.
  3. Deep-fry until golden brown and cooked through.

Nasi Lemak Rendang Daging

A rich and tender beef rendang recipe that perfectly complements the coconut rice. Ideal for special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • Beef, cut into cubes
  • Coconut milk, kerisik (toasted coconut paste)
  • Rendang paste (chilies, lemongrass, galangal, turmeric, ginger, shallots, garlic)
  • Kaffir lime leaves, turmeric leaves

Instructions:

  1. Sauté rendang paste until fragrant.
  2. Add beef and cook until browned.
  3. Add coconut milk, kerisik, and leaves; simmer until gravy thickens and oil separates.

Nasi Lemak Sotong Kangkung

A delightful seafood variation featuring stir-fried squid and water spinach in a savory sauce. Quick to prepare and full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh squid, cleaned and sliced
  • Kangkung (water spinach)
  • Chili paste, garlic, shallots, belacan, tamarind paste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté blended chili paste with garlic, shallots, belacan.
  2. Add squid and cook briefly.
  3. Stir in kangkung and tamarind paste until kangkung is wilted.

Nasi Lemak Sambal Kerang

For seafood enthusiasts, this recipe features cockles cooked in a rich and spicy sambal. A truly authentic Malaysian experience.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh cockles, blanched and shelled
  • Sambal paste (dried chilies, shallots, garlic, belacan)
  • Tamarind paste, sugar, salt

Instructions:

  1. Sauté sambal paste until fragrant.
  2. Add cockles, tamarind paste, sugar, and salt.
  3. Stir-fry until cockles are well coated and cooked through.

Nasi Lemak Vegetarian

A delicious plant-based Nasi Lemak, substituting traditional meats with flavorful vegetarian alternatives. Healthy and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • Coconut rice (as per basic recipe)
  • Vegetarian sambal (chilies, tomatoes, onions, vegetarian belacan)
  • Accompaniments: Fried tofu, tempeh, long beans, vegetarian ikan bilis

Instructions:

  1. Prepare coconut rice.
  2. Make vegetarian sambal.
  3. Fry tofu, tempeh, and other vegetarian accompaniments.
  4. Assemble the dish.
